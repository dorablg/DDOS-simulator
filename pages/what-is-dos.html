What is a DoS Attack?

A Denial of Service (DoS) attack is a cyber attack designed to make a computer or network resource unavailable to its intended users by overwhelming it with traffic or requests.

Types of DoS Attacks

Volume-Based Attacks

Floods the network with a huge amount of traffic to overwhelm bandwidth.

  • UDP floods
  • ICMP floods
  • Other spoofed-packet floods

Protocol Attacks

Exploits weaknesses in protocol stack layers 3 and 4.

  • SYN floods
  • Fragmented packet attacks
  • Ping of Death

Application Layer Attacks

Targets web application packets to crash the web server.

  • HTTP floods
  • Slowloris
  • GET/POST floods

Impact of DoS Attacks

Financial Loss

Businesses can lose revenue due to service downtime and recovery costs.

Reputation Damage

Customer trust may be affected by service unavailability.

Resource Drain

IT teams must divert resources to mitigate and prevent attacks.

Data Loss

Some attacks can lead to data corruption or loss.

Historical Timeline

1988

Morris Worm

One of the first recognized DoS attacks, caused by a program that overwhelmed early internet computers.

2000

MafiaBoy Attacks

Major DDoS attacks against Amazon, CNN, eBay, and Yahoo.

2007

Estonia Cyber Attacks

First known case of a coordinated cyberattack against a nation state.

2016

Mirai Botnet

Massive DDoS attack using IoT devices, affecting major internet platforms.
